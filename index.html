A "black vinyl record" typically refers to gramophone records that are produced in a distinct black color and are used for recording sound in the 78 rpm format in the early 20th century.The term "black vinyl record" refers to the recording process of the sound that is different from that of the traditional vinyl records. Black vinyl records are marked with extreme grooves for analog recording and are 10 inches in length.Black vinyl records were used by many music artists for many years, but in the 1950s, many gramophones switched to 33 and 45 rpm vinyl records, leading to a decline in the use of black vinyl records.Today, black vinyl records are highly valued by many music collectors and are considered important landmarks in music history.

Gramophones

A gramophone is a device that plays and amplifies sound, typically using vinyl records or gramophone discs. The sound quality of a gramophone record is considered analog, which means that information is inscribed onto the record in an analog manner, different from the digital encoding used in electronic formats.

The process of creating sound from a gramophone record involves physically etching grooves into the record during the recording process. The grooves translate the sound into a musical signal and provide for its analog inscription. Gramophones were popular in the 19th and 20th centuries and served as a valuable medium for playing music.
